A GAME OF YESTERDAY AND THE FUTURE: SQUASH!

You might have heard them saying:
Janis is a sensei of Squash
and probably they are not wrong.

Janis - head coach for the Ladies Squash Club of Valencia for many years.

Squash, a high-intensity racquet sport, has gained recognition for its remarkable health advantages. According to Forbes magazine, this physically demanding sport offers a multitude of benefits that set it apart from other forms of physical activity.

One of the standout advantages of squash is its exceptional cardiovascular benefits. The fast-paced, dynamic nature of the game keeps players constantly on the move, leading to an elevated heart rate and increased oxygen consumption. This not only enhances cardiovascular fitness but also improves lung capacity and overall endurance. Squash can be compared to sports like running, cycling, or swimming in terms of cardiovascular exertion. A study cited by Forbes found that squash players can burn up to 1000 calories in an hour of play, making it one of the most calorie-burning sports.

Furthermore, squash is a fantastic sport for developing muscular strength and endurance. The rapid changes in direction, powerful shots, and continuous rallies engage a wide range of muscle groups, particularly in the legs, core, and upper body. This results in stronger muscles and improved flexibility. Squash also hones agility, speed, and hand-eye coordination.

Another noteworthy health advantage of squash is its potential to help with weight management. The high energy expenditure during a game, combined with the fun and engaging nature of the sport, encourages regular physical activity. It can also be a superb way to relieve stress and improve mental health as the focus required to track and react to the ball provides a mental workout as well.

In summary, according to Forbes magazine, squash is a sport that offers numerous health benefits, making it an excellent choice for those seeking a physically challenging and mentally stimulating activity. With its cardiovascular benefits, muscular strength and endurance development, and potential for weight management, squash distinguishes itself as a sport that's on par with the most rigorous physical activities. So, whether you're an experienced player or a novice, consider giving squash a try to harness its remarkable health advantages.
